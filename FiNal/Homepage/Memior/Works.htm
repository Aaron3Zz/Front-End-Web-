Aaron Zhan

  • "A Memoir"

  • Material Choice

  • Paper

  • Thread

  • Installation

  • Artist Statement

  • I believe memories are consumable and relatively sparse products. That is to say,
    when we consume our memories for certain times, they would disappear. Then, how
    can we prove the existence of those subject matters in of our memories. Although our
    physical body or our conscious minds are influenced by those memories, we can still
    not have the clear imagery of our past. I intend to create a photo-sculpture, which
    have a similar formation like booklet, to let audiences themselves to experience the
    process of “unfolding” memories.

  • Fabrication

  • In order to create this photo-
    sculpture, I chose to utilize materials
    like thread, needles, acrylic and
    paper.I would believe that thread is
    naturally embedded with the idea of
    recovery, maybe because of its
    unique usage. Also, I think paper and
    thread are a perfect match for
    conveying a certain kind of feelings,
    especially melancholy.

  • Final Notes

  • Hopefully, I have created the process of “unfolding” our
    memories and grasped the vague feelings of lives.
    Thank you for viewing!

  • I sew photos that I took in the past
    and construct this piece that could
    be easily folded and unfolded.
